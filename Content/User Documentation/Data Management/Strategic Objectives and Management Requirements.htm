Strategic Objectives and Management Requirements

Each administrative unit and agency has a set of Strategic Objectives and Management Requirements related to the landscape and its resources. These objectives and requirements guide decision-making for the unit, including fire decisions. This planning direction is derived from NEPA-approved documents that include Land, Resource, and/or Fire Management Plans. Strategic Objectives and Management Requirements can be added to WFDSS as textual and/or spatial data, depending on the WFDSS Planning Process chosen by the administrative unit.

Data Managers pre-load the Strategic Objectives and Management Requirements for their administrative unit to ensure that incident managers are using this information as part of their incident decision-making process. Strategic Objectives and Management Requirements are included in the decision once a planning area is created or FMUs are added manually. Incident-level objectives and requirements developed for an incident should be based on these Strategic Objectives and Management Requirements.
