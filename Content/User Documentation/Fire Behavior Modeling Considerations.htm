Fire Behavior Modeling Considerations

Users need to update fire scars and other disturbances after 2016.

  • Either the Finney (1998) or Scott and Reinhardt (2001) (https://www.fire.org/downloads/nexus/2.0.0/rp29.pdf) crown fire method (CFM) is suitable for the initial analysis. If you use Finney, you will likely need to double or triple the CBD. This IS NOT necessary when using Scott/Reinhardt. It is also a good practice to critique the CBH when using either CFC method.
  • The Scott and Reinhardt CFM likely produces more spotting, higher rates of spread, and flame lengths, and more passive and active crown fire.
  • Users are encouraged to download the LCP critique in WFDSS to evaluate the fire behavior fuel models and canopy attributes that will affect predicted fire behavior.
