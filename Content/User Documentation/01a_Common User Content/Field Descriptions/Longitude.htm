Longitude

The angular distance, expressed in degrees, minutes, and seconds of a point on the earth’s surface east or west of a prime meridian (usually the Greenwich Meridian). All lines of longitude are great circles that intersect the equator and pass through the North and South poles. (GSTOP) For the US, valid longitudes range from about W67-W126 for the lower 48 states, W153-W160 for Hawaii, and W130-E172 for Alaska.
